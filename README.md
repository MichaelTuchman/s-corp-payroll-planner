# Solo S-Corp Payroll & Cash Planner

> **Can I safely run this payroll?**

That is the question this planner is built to answer.

Many S-corp tools help answer questions such as:

- What is a reasonable salary?
- How much can I contribute to a retirement plan?
- How can I reduce my taxes?

Those are important questions.

But once you've chosen a salary, another question remains:

> **Given my revenue, payroll, taxes, and retirement elections, what happens to my business cash if I run payroll today?**

This planner is designed to answer that operational question.

Rather than optimizing a tax strategy, it models the complete cash impact of a proposed payroll before payday.

---

# The Problem

Imagine you're a solo consultant operating as an S corporation.

You invoice your clients monthly.

You pay yourself through payroll.

You contribute to a retirement plan.

At the end of the month, you need to decide how to run payroll.

Your CPA may have recommended an annual salary.

Your retirement advisor may have suggested a Solo 401(k), SEP-IRA, or SIMPLE IRA.

But none of those recommendations answer questions like:

- Can I comfortably afford this payroll?
- What if revenue is lower this month?
- What if I increase my salary?
- What if I contribute more to retirement?
- How much cash remains after payroll?
- Am I creating a cash problem without realizing it?

Those are operational cash-planning questions.

This planner is built specifically to answer them.

---

# What the Planner Models

Each payroll scenario models the complete flow of money through your business, including:

- Available business cash
- Gross wages
- Federal withholding
- Social Security
- Medicare
- Employer payroll taxes
- FUTA
- Retirement contributions
- Remaining business cash

Rather than showing isolated calculations, the planner models payroll as one connected cash flow.

---

# Compare Scenarios, Not Just Numbers

Real payroll decisions rarely come down to a single calculation.

Instead, business owners ask questions like:

- Should I contribute to a Solo 401(k), SEP-IRA, or SIMPLE IRA?
- What happens if I increase my salary by $5,000?
- How much cash remains if I work fewer billable hours this month?
- Should I make a retirement contribution now or wait until the next payroll?

The planner is designed to answer these questions by letting you create and compare multiple payroll scenarios side by side.

Each scenario represents a complete payroll decision—including wages, taxes, retirement contributions, and remaining business cash—allowing you to evaluate trade-offs before committing to payroll.

Rather than asking:

> "What's the right answer?"

the planner helps answer:

> **"Which payroll scenario leaves my business in the healthiest cash position while still meeting my compensation and retirement goals?"**

# Retirement Plans Supported

The planner currently supports:

- Solo 401(k)
- SEP-IRA
- SIMPLE IRA
- No retirement plan

For SIMPLE IRAs, both IRS-approved employer contribution methods are supported:

- Dollar-for-dollar matching contributions
- Fixed nonelective employer contributions

The planner correctly models the different tax treatment of employee and employer retirement contributions, including the distinct mechanics of Solo 401(k) employee deferrals versus employer contributions.

Age-50 catch-up contributions are supported where applicable.

---

# Payroll Threshold Tracking

The planner tracks important payroll thresholds using year-to-date wages, including:

- Social Security wage base
- FUTA wage base
- Additional Medicare threshold

This allows payroll calculations to remain accurate throughout the year.
