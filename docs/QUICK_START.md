# Quick Start

## 1. Open the workbook

Open:

```text
workbook/Solo_S_Corp_Payroll_Cash_Planner.xlsx
```

Microsoft Excel is the primary supported platform. The workbook avoids macros and external data connections.

## 2. Enter Section 1 inputs

Update the blue cells for the payroll scenario:

- planning month;
- planned billable hours;
- billing rate;
- wage rate;
- expected client receipts;
- beginning LLC cash;
- operating expenses;
- payroll-service fees;
- minimum operating cash reserve;
- SEP contribution rate;
- year-to-date wages;
- year-to-date SEP contributions; and
- notes.

## 3. Review Section 2 tax assumptions

Replace the default tax rates and annual limits with figures supplied by the accountant or payroll provider. State, local, unemployment, disability, and leave-program assumptions vary by jurisdiction.

## 4. Review the model results

The model calculates:

- gross wages;
- employee withholding;
- net employee paycheck;
- employer payroll obligations;
- required deposits and reserves;
- total payroll cash requirement;
- cash after obligations;
- available cash; and
- Cash Health Status.

## 5. Reconcile before running payroll

Use the model for planning. After the accountant or payroll provider runs payroll, compare the actual paystub and required deposits with the model. Actual payroll documents are authoritative.

## 6. Preserve a monthly snapshot

The horizontal snapshot at the bottom is designed for a future value-only payroll register. Until an automated recording feature exists, copy the snapshot and paste values into a separate record if desired.
