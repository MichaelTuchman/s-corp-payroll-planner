# Frequently Asked Questions

## Does this workbook run payroll?

No. It models payroll and cash obligations. An accountant or payroll provider should run the actual payroll.

## Is this for every type of business?

No. It is designed specifically for a single-owner S corporation with one W-2 employee, the owner.

## Does it calculate reasonable compensation?

No. It displays wage, billing, and cash metrics that can support a discussion with a tax adviser. It does not determine whether compensation is reasonable under IRS standards.

## Why are billed revenue and expected receipts separate?

Billed revenue reflects work performed and invoiced. Expected receipts reflect cash entering the business account. For prompt-paying clients, they may be identical.

## Why are state and local rates entered manually?

Payroll rules vary by jurisdiction, employer-specific unemployment rate, locality, owner/officer treatment, and paid-leave or disability programs. Manual assumptions keep the model transparent and avoid false precision.

## Does a traditional SEP-IRA have an employee contribution?

A traditional SEP-IRA is funded through employer contributions. It does not provide employee elective salary deferrals like a 401(k).

## Why is the historical register not included yet?

The first release focuses on the planning model. A future register will receive copied values only, preserving historical payroll facts even when formulas or assumptions later change.

## Is Google Sheets supported?

Microsoft Excel is the primary platform. The workbook may import into Google Sheets, but equal feature and formatting support is not currently promised.
